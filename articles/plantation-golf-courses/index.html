Plantation Golf Courses: A Dad's Guide to a Few Hours of Peace

The Dad Reality Check

Look, I'm not going to pretend that golf is a escape hatch from family life. It's not. But I'll be honest with you—when you're juggling work deadlines, fixing that leak under the sink, refereeing arguments about screen time, and trying to remember what your wife's favorite pizza toppings are, sometimes you need three to four hours to yourself. Enter Plantation's golf scene.

Plantation, Florida is blessed with several solid golf courses that aren't so fancy you feel like you need to dust off your khakis from 2009, and they're not so casual that you're playing through a foursome of bachelor parties every Saturday morning. They're right in that sweet spot where a dad can actually focus on his game, clear his head, and come back home a slightly better version of himself—or at least one who's smiled in the last few hours.

I've spent enough time on these greens to know which courses are worth your time and which ones have too many gotchas. Here's what I've learned about getting those precious hours on the course.

The Courses Worth Your Saturday Morning

Jacaranda Golf Club

Address: 9200 W. Broward Boulevard, Plantation, FL 33324

Green fees typically sit around $30-$45 before noon, which leaves your wallet less angry when you get home. Pro tip: go early. 7 AM tee time, and you're home by noon to actually have a day with your family.

The Practical Stuff That Actually Matters

Here's what separates a good golf trip from one that leaves you more stressed than when you started:

Booking: Call ahead. Don't just show up. I learned this the hard way on a Thursday afternoon when the course was closed for some golf charity event I'd never heard of. Most courses in Plantation take reservations up to a week in advance, and they'll hold your spot if you call before 9 AM the morning of.

Best times: Weekday mornings are your golden ticket. Tuesday through Friday, 7-9 AM, you'll get a fast course with minimal waiting. Weekends are busy, and if you go Saturday afternoon, you're looking at 5+ hour rounds. Nobody came home for a 5-hour round.

Bring what matters: Your game improved roughly 300% when I stopped trying to keep score on my phone. Grab a scorecard, grab a pencil, and just focus on the shot in front of you. Leave the email at home. Your boss will survive without you for 3.5 hours.

Cash or card: Both courses take cards, but bring cash for tips. The rangers, grounds crew, and marshals appreciate it, and you appreciate getting faster pace of play.

The Real Payoff

Look, I'm not going to tell you that golf solves your problems. The sink still leaks when you get home. The kids still need driving to soccer practice. Work emails are still annoying. But something happens when you're out there for those hours—you stop thinking about all of it. Your shoulders drop. You breathe a little deeper. You remember that you're actually pretty good at this one thing, even if you're just an okay golfer.

And here's the part nobody talks about: you come home better. Lighter. More patient. Genuinely more interested in what your wife did that morning. That version of you that's been out in the sun for three hours is a better husband and father than the version that's been staring at a screen all week.

Bottom line: Plantation's golf courses aren't fancy country clubs that require a membership or a trust fund. They're accessible, well-run courses that respect your time and give you exactly what you need—a few hours to yourself, a chance to focus on something you enjoy, and permission to take care of yourself. Schedule your tee time, show up early, and let the rest of the world wait. You've earned it.
